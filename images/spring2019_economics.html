Marginalism

One must analyze the additional or incremental costs and benefits arising from a choice and decision

  1. Marginal cost(MC)= incremental costs
  2. Marginal benefit(MB)= bebefits
  3. Economic decision rule
    • MB>MC ⇨ pursue that activity
    • MC>MB ⇨ Don't pursue the activity

Opportunity cost

The valuse of the next best alternitive that we forgo, or give up when we make a decision.

Opportunity cost is explicit cost + implicit cost

  • Explicit cost: out-of-poket expenditure. Money that is coming out of the bank directly.
  • Implicit cost: hidden expenditures like tradeoff.

Incentives are rewards and penalites that motivate behavior.

Example

The Scope of Economics

  • Microeconomics: focuses on individual decision-making agents such as individual, households, and firms.
  • Macroeconomics: focuses on the aggregates or economy-wide issues.

Microeconomics versus Macroeconomics

Positive and Normative Economics

  • Positive economics: Analysis that tries to answer questions about the way the world works, which have definite right or wrong answers.(No judgement)
  • Normative economics: Analysis that involves how the world should work.(judgement)
